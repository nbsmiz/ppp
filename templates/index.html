Paycheck Protection Program Loan Distribution

Is the federal government helping keep you and your community whole through the pandemic?

In response to increasing unemployment due to shut downs in repoonse to the COVID-19 pandemic, the federal government provided a number of responses. One of these was the $669-billion Paycheck Protection Program, intended to provide loans to small businesses to facilitate the retention of empoloyees during a time of suppressed economic activity.

This is a lot of money to someone working one of the jobs this programs was purported to be intended to save. There are someone import questions to ask though:

  1. Were any jobs saved?
  2. How much did it cost to save these jobs?
  3. Perhaps most important for an individual, did your community receive any assistance?

If you want to jump to checking out loans in your community, click here for a searchable map with the highest-value loans mapped and search for any zipcode, city, state or address.

Following below, we'll walk through a series of plots looking at the distribution of loans disbursed as part of the PPP. Each image is also a link an interactive Plotly plot.

Looking at where the loans went isn't all that surprising, with California, Texas, New York and Florida receiving the largest total value of loans, with California at over $50 billion.

Digging deeper, we see a more equitable distribution across the states according to per capita loan-dollar disbursements, where, while New York is still near the top, North Dakota received the highest loan-dollar per capita, compared to being ninth in total loan dollars.
